Nourishing Communities

Good nutrition is the foundation for healthy development, learning, and economic productivity. Our Nutrition Programs address malnutrition in all its forms, with a special focus on children under five, pregnant and lactating women, and other vulnerable groups.

We combine direct nutritional support with education and sustainable food systems to create lasting improvements in community nutrition.

"When children receive proper nutrition in their first 1000 days, they gain a foundation for lifelong health, learning, and prosperity."

Program Components

Our comprehensive approach to nutrition includes:

Nutrition Screening

Regular screening of children and women to identify malnutrition early.

Supplementation

Vitamin A, iron, and micronutrient powders for children and mothers.

Treatment of Malnutrition

Therapeutic feeding for severely malnourished children.

Breastfeeding Support

Counseling and support for exclusive breastfeeding.

Nutrition Education

Cooking demonstrations and lessons on balanced diets.

Food Security

Linking nutrition with agriculture for sustainable food access.

Nutrition Services

We provide these essential nutrition services to vulnerable groups.

Child Nutrition

Growth monitoring, complementary feeding support, and treatment for acute malnutrition.

Maternal Nutrition

Nutrition support during pregnancy and lactation, including iron-folate supplementation.

Infant Feeding

Promotion of exclusive breastfeeding and appropriate complementary feeding.

Micronutrient Programs

Vitamin A supplementation, deworming, and multiple micronutrient powders.

Growth Monitoring

Regular weighing and measurement to track child growth and development.

Cooking Demos

Hands-on sessions showing how to prepare nutritious meals with local ingredients.

Key Interventions

How we address malnutrition in communities.

MUAC Screening

Community-based screening using Mid-Upper Arm Circumference to identify malnutrition early.

Prevention

Supplementary Feeding

Providing nutrient-dense foods to moderately malnourished children and women.

Treatment

Therapeutic Feeding

Outpatient treatment for severe acute malnutrition with ready-to-use therapeutic foods.

Critical Care

Mother-to-Mother Support

Peer support groups where mothers share experiences and learn together.
